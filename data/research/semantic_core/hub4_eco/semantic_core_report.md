# Semantic Core Research Report: Hub 4 - Sustainable Living (ecovodalife)

## 1. Overview
This report details the semantic core research for Hub 4, focusing on Sustainable Living. The goal is to provide a comprehensive keyword tree that supports content creation across three languages: English (EN), Russian (RU), and Serbian (SR).

### Hub 4 Focus Areas:
- **Zero-Waste**: Reducing waste to zero, focusing on the "5 R's" (Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Rot).
- **Plastic-Free**: Eliminating single-use plastics and finding sustainable alternatives.
- **Eco-friendly Home**: Implementing sustainable design, non-toxic materials, and energy efficiency.
- **Carbon Footprint**: Understanding and reducing individual and household greenhouse gas emissions.

---

## 2. Keyword Strategy
The semantic core is structured as a tree to cover the full user journey:
- **High-tail (Head Keywords)**: Broad terms with high volume, used for pillar pages.
- **Mid-tail (Category Keywords)**: More specific terms that define sub-topics and categories.
- **Long-tail/LSI (Specific/Intent-driven)**: Highly specific queries, often in question form, targeting low-volume but high-conversion traffic (informational and commercial intent).

## 4. Language Adaptation Notes
- **Russian (RU)**: Strong interest in "осознанное потребление" (conscious consumption) and "эко-привычки" (eco-habits). Terminology often mixes English loans (Zero Waste) with descriptive Russian phrases.
- **Serbian (SR)**: Growing trend towards "održivost" (sustainability). Focus on local bulk shops ("prodavnice na rinfuz") and traditional methods of reuse.
- **English (EN)**: Highly competitive. Focus on "lsi keywords" and "topic clusters" to gain authority in niche sub-sectors like "plastic-free bathroom".
